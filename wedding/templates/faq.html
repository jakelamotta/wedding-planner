{{gettext('How to get there?')}}
The easiset way, travelling from outside of Sweden or Finland, is to fly to Arlanda Airport or Skavsta Airport. We suggest Arlanda because it is closer and there are more flights.
From Arlanda you can either rent a car or take the train (40 Uppsala C) towards Uppsala. In Uppsala you should transfer to the Eckerö line bus which final destination is the port of Grisslehamn, the bus leaves from Uppsala Resecentrum/Stationsgatan hpl E7. The bus is included in the ferry ticket. The ferry to the Åland Islands departs three times a day. At 10am, 3pm and 8pm. For more information see here.
If flying to Skavsta the best option is to take FlixBus or Flygbussarna to the Central station of Stockholm and take the corresponding Eckerö line bus from there.
What to do in the Åland islands?
Except for the actual wedding there will be other activities for guests arriving early but details regarding this will be presented when we get closer to the big day. There are also plenty of tourist destinations to visit while in Åland, perhaps a visit to the Castle of Kastelholm. For the beer enthusiast the gastropub and brewery Stallhagen has a wide variety of Åland brewed beer and good food. For the guests that can't get enough of travelling by boat a trip with Sunnan II could be a good alternative. Please see Visit Aland for more information on what to do in Åland.
How is the weather?
While the average temperature in June is 18° the weather in the Åland Islands can vary so make sure to bring clothes for different types of weather. As we say in Sweden, there are no bad weather, just bad clothing.
What about covid-19?
Finland, as most countries has had different travel restrictions on and off. We don't forsee any problem with entering Finland in June. Hopefully the situation is improved when summer comes but here you can find updated travel restrictions for Åland. Please keep yourself updated and ask us if you have any questions at all. As there will be around 60-80 guests attending the reception and some are in risk groups we encourage all guests to get vaccinated. This will also alleviate issues with travelling.
